# Tectonic Controls on Lithium Mineralization

## Overview

Lithium deposits form in three fundamentally different tectonic settings, each with distinct geodynamic drivers:

1. **LCT Pegmatites (Hard-rock)**: Post-orogenic extensional settings with highly fractionated S-type granites
2. **Brine Deposits (Salt lakes)**: Continental rift/plateau basins with volcanic activity and arid climate
3. **Clay Deposits (Sedimentary)**: Extensional basins with volcanic ash alteration in alkaline lacustrine environments

Understanding these tectonic controls is critical for:
- Regional-scale exploration targeting
- Deposit-scale structural controls
- Genetic model development
- Resource potential assessment

## Part I: LCT Pegmatite Tectonic Settings

### 1.1 Orogenic Cycle and Pegmatite Formation

**Tectonic Evolution Sequence**:
```
Continental Collision → Peak Metamorphism → Post-orogenic Extension → Crustal Melting → Pegmatite Emplacement
    (T₀)                  (T₀+10-30 Ma)        (T₀+20-50 Ma)          (T₀+30-60 Ma)      (T₀+35-65 Ma)
```

**Key Tectonic Stages**:

1. **Syn-collision Stage** (T₀ to T₀+20 Ma)
   - Crustal thickening (40-70 km)
   - High-pressure metamorphism (0.8-1.5 GPa)
   - Dehydration of metasediments
   - Initial crustal melting at depth

2. **Post-orogenic Extension Stage** (T₀+20 to T₀+50 Ma)
   - Lithospheric delamination or slab break-off
   - Asthenospheric upwelling
   - Crustal thinning and heating
   - Widespread S-type granite magmatism
   - **This is the critical window for LCT pegmatite formation**

3. **Pegmatite Emplacement Stage** (T₀+30 to T₀+65 Ma)
   - Late-stage granite fractionation
   - Volatile-rich residual melts
   - Emplacement along extensional structures
   - Rapid crystallization in shallow crust (<5 km)

**Geodynamic Drivers**:
- **Thermal relaxation**: Post-orogenic heating of thickened crust
- **Decompression melting**: Crustal thinning triggers partial melting
- **Volatile flux**: Water-rich metasediments provide flux for melting
- **Extensional tectonics**: Creates space for magma ascent and pegmatite emplacement

### 1.2 Source Rock Requirements

**Optimal Source Lithologies**:
1. **Metasedimentary sequences** (pelites, greywackes)
   - High Al₂O₃/CaO ratio (>3)
   - Enriched in Li, Cs, Rb, Be
   - Muscovite-bearing assemblages

2. **S-type granite protoliths**
   - Peraluminous composition (A/CNK >1.1)
   - Two-mica granites (muscovite + biotite)
   - High degree of fractionation (SiO₂ >72%)

**Geochemical Indicators of Fertile Source**:
- Li/Y >5
- Rb/Sr >10
- Cs/Rb >0.1
- Nb/Ta <5 (strong Ta enrichment)
- P₂O₅ >0.3% (phosphate saturation)

### 1.3 Structural Controls on Pegmatite Emplacement

**Regional-Scale Structures**:

1. **Major Shear Zones**
   - Provide conduits for magma ascent
   - Control pegmatite field distribution
   - Examples: Greenbushes Shear Zone (Australia), La Grande Shear Zone (Canada)

2. **Extensional Fault Systems**
   - Create dilatational jogs for pegmatite emplacement
   - Control pegmatite orientation and geometry
   - Typical spacing: 100 m to 10 km

3. **Fold Hinges and Axial Planes**
   - Preferential sites for pegmatite injection
   - Control pegmatite swarm geometry

**Deposit-Scale Structures**:

1. **Brittle-Ductile Transition Zone** (300-450°C)
   - Optimal depth for pegmatite emplacement
   - Allows fracture propagation and melt injection

2. **Dilational Structures**
   - Tension gashes
   - Pull-apart structures
   - Boudin necks

3. **Lithological Contacts**
   - Competency contrasts focus stress
   - Greenstone-granite contacts (Greenbushes)
   - Schist-gneiss contacts (Pilgangoora)

**Emplacement Mechanisms**:
- **Dyke injection**: Vertical to sub-vertical sheets along fractures
- **Sill injection**: Sub-horizontal sheets along bedding/foliation
- **Replacement**: Metasomatic replacement of host rocks (rare)

### 1.4 Magmatic Fractionation and Li Enrichment

**Fractionation Sequence**:
```
Parent Granite → Aplite → Pegmatite → LCT Pegmatite
  (Li: 20 ppm)   (50 ppm)  (200 ppm)   (1000-5000 ppm)
```

**Critical Fractionation Processes**:

1. **Crystal Fractionation**
   - Removal of plagioclase, K-feldspar, biotite
   - Incompatible elements (Li, Cs, Ta, Be) concentrate in melt
   - Degree of fractionation: >95% crystallization

2. **Volatile Saturation**
   - H₂O, F, B, P enrichment in residual melt
   - Lowers solidus temperature
   - Enhances Li solubility and transport

3. **Immiscibility (debated)**
   - Possible silicate-aqueous fluid separation
   - Li partitions into aqueous phase
   - May explain extreme Li enrichment in some deposits

**Zoning Patterns in LCT Pegmatites**:
```
Border Zone → Wall Zone → Intermediate Zone → Core Zone
(barren)      (low Li)    (spodumene-rich)    (lepidolite, petalite)
```

## Part II: Global Hard-Rock Lithium Provinces

### 2.1 Yilgarn Craton, Western Australia

**Tectonic Setting**:
- **Craton age**: Archean (3.7-2.6 Ga)
- **Pegmatite age**: 2.65-2.60 Ga (late Archean)
- **Tectonic event**: Post-orogenic extension after Yilgarn Orogeny

**Regional Geology**:
- Granite-greenstone terrane
- Bimodal volcanism (mafic-felsic)
- Extensive S-type granite intrusions
- Major shear zones (NNW-trending)

**Key Deposits and Structural Controls**:

1. **Greenbushes** (World's largest hard-rock Li mine)
   - Host: Archean greenstone belt
   - Structure: NNW-trending shear zone
   - Pegmatite geometry: 3.5 km long, 50-250 m wide, >400 m depth
   - Emplacement control: Greenstone-granite contact + shear zone
   - Grade: 2.0-2.5% Li₂O (spodumene + lepidolite)

2. **Pilgangoora**
   - Host: Pilgangoora Syncline (greenstone)
   - Structure: Fold hinge + shear zone
   - Pegmatite swarm: Multiple parallel dykes
   - Grade: 1.2-1.5% Li₂O

3. **Mt Marion**
   - Host: Greenstone-granite contact
   - Structure: NNW-trending fault zone
   - Pegmatite geometry: Steeply dipping dyke swarm
   - Grade: 1.3-1.5% Li₂O

**Exploration Model**:
- Target: Archean greenstone belts adjacent to S-type granites
- Structural control: NNW-trending shear zones
- Geochemical pathfinder: Li-Cs-Ta-Be anomalies in soils
- Geophysical signature: Magnetic lows (pegmatite vs. greenstone)

### 2.2 Superior Craton, Canada (James Bay / Abitibi)

**Tectonic Setting**:
- **Craton age**: Archean (3.0-2.6 Ga)
- **Pegmatite age**: 2.7-2.6 Ga
- **Tectonic event**: Post-orogenic extension after Kenoran Orogeny

**Regional Geology**:
- Granite-greenstone terrane (similar to Yilgarn)
- La Grande Subprovince (James Bay)
- Abitibi Subprovince (Quebec-Ontario)
- Extensive pegmatite fields

**Key Deposits**:

1. **Whabouchi** (James Bay, Quebec)
   - Host: Archean metasediments
   - Structure: Regional fault zone
   - Pegmatite geometry: 1.5 km long, 100-150 m wide
   - Grade: 1.4-1.6% Li₂O (spodumene)

2. **Rose** (James Bay, Quebec)
   - Host: Greenstone-granite contact
   - Structure: Shear zone-controlled
   - Pegmatite swarm: Multiple dykes
   - Grade: 1.0-1.3% Li₂O

3. **Corvette** (James Bay, Quebec)
   - Host: Metasedimentary sequence
   - Structure: Fold-controlled
   - Pegmatite geometry: Sill-like bodies
   - Grade: 1.2-1.5% Li₂O

**Exploration Model**:
- Target: Archean greenstone-granite contacts
- Structural control: Regional fault zones (E-W to NE-SW)
- Geochemical pathfinder: Li-Cs-Ta in lake sediments
- Geophysical signature: EM conductors (graphitic shears)

### 2.3 Kibaran Belt, Central Africa

**Tectonic Setting**:
- **Belt age**: Mesoproterozoic (1.4-0.95 Ga)
- **Pegmatite age**: 1.0-0.95 Ga
- **Tectonic event**: Kibaran Orogeny (intracontinental collision)

**Regional Geology**:
- Intracratonic rift basin (Congo Craton margin)
- Metasedimentary sequences (pelites, quartzites)
- Syn- to post-tectonic S-type granites
- Extensive pegmatite fields (Sn-Ta-Li)

**Key Deposits**:

1. **Manono** (DRC) — World's largest undeveloped Li resource
   - Host: Kibaran metasediments
   - Structure: Rift basin margin
   - Pegmatite geometry: 13 km long, 200-500 m wide
   - Grade: 1.6-1.8% Li₂O (spodumene)
   - Resource: >400 Mt @ 1.65% Li₂O

2. **Arcadia** (Zimbabwe)
   - Host: Archean basement + Kibaran overprint
   - Structure: Shear zone-controlled
   - Pegmatite geometry: Multiple dykes
   - Grade: 1.2-1.4% Li₂O

3. **Bikita** (Zimbabwe)
   - Host: Archean greenstone
   - Structure: Fold hinge + shear zone
   - Pegmatite geometry: Complex zoned body
   - Grade: 1.0-1.5% Li₂O (petalite + lepidolite)

**Exploration Model**:
- Target: Kibaran metasedimentary belts
- Structural control: Rift basin margins + shear zones
- Geochemical pathfinder: Li-Cs-Ta-Sn anomalies
- Geophysical signature: Gravity lows (pegmatite vs. host)

### 2.4 Songpan-Ganzi Orogenic Belt, China (Western Sichuan - Eastern Tibet)

**Tectonic Setting**:
- **Belt age**: Triassic (250-200 Ma)
- **Pegmatite age**: 220-200 Ma (Indosinian Orogeny)
- **Tectonic event**: Collision of North China, South China, and Qiangtang blocks

**Regional Geology**:
- Thick Triassic flysch sequences (turbidites)
- Syn- to post-collisional S-type granites
- Two-mica granite belt (Markam-Songpan)
- Extensive pegmatite fields (Li-Be-Nb-Ta)

**Key Deposits**:

1. **Jiajika** (Sichuan) — China's largest Li deposit
   - Host: Triassic metasediments
   - Structure: Fold-controlled pegmatite swarm
   - Pegmatite geometry: 50+ dykes, 500-2000 m long
   - Grade: 1.2-1.5% Li₂O (spodumene)
   - Resource: >200 Mt @ 1.3% Li₂O

2. **Ke'eryin** (Sichuan)
   - Host: Two-mica granite
   - Structure: Granite cupola + fracture zone
   - Pegmatite geometry: Stockwork + dykes
   - Grade: 1.0-1.3% Li₂O

**Tectonic Evolution**:
```
T₀: Triassic collision (240-230 Ma)
T₁: Peak metamorphism (230-220 Ma)
T₂: Post-orogenic extension (220-210 Ma) → Two-mica granite intrusion
T₃: Pegmatite emplacement (215-200 Ma)
```

**Exploration Model**:
- Target: Two-mica granite belts in Triassic flysch
- Structural control: Fold hinges + extensional faults
- Geochemical pathfinder: Li-Rb-Cs in stream sediments
- Geophysical signature: Magnetic lows + gravity lows

### 2.5 Other Significant Hard-Rock Provinces

**Carolina Tin-Spodumene Belt, USA**:
- **Age**: Paleozoic (380-350 Ma, Alleghanian Orogeny)
- **Host**: Metasediments + S-type granites
- **Deposits**: Kings Mountain, Piedmont (historical producers)
- **Grade**: 1.0-1.5% Li₂O

**Cinovec, Czech Republic**:
- **Age**: Variscan (320-310 Ma)
- **Host**: Granite cupola
- **Deposit type**: Greisen-hosted (zinnwaldite mica)
- **Grade**: 0.3-0.4% Li₂O (lower grade, but large tonnage)
- **Resource**: >370 Mt @ 0.38% Li₂O

**Keliber, Finland**:
- **Age**: Paleoproterozoic (1.85-1.80 Ga, Svecofennian Orogeny)
- **Host**: Metasediments + S-type granites
- **Deposits**: Multiple pegmatite dykes
- **Grade**: 1.0-1.3% Li₂O

## Part III: Brine-Type Lithium Deposits — Tectonic Controls

### 3.1 South American Lithium Triangle — Geodynamic Framework

**Plate Tectonic Setting**:
- **Subduction zone**: Nazca Plate subducting beneath South American Plate
- **Subduction rate**: 6-7 cm/year (eastward)
- **Slab dip**: Shallow (10-30°) in flat-slab segments
- **Volcanic arc**: Active Andean arc (Quaternary to Recent)

**Crustal Architecture**:
- **Crustal thickness**: 60-70 km (Altiplano-Puna)
- **Moho depth**: Deepest in central Andes
- **Lithospheric mantle**: Partially delaminated
- **Asthenospheric upwelling**: Beneath Puna plateau

**Key Geodynamic Processes**:

1. **Plateau Uplift** (10-6 Ma)
   - Crustal shortening + magmatic addition
   - Surface elevation: 3500-4500 m
   - Creates orographic barrier (rain shadow effect)

2. **Volcanic Activity** (Quaternary)
   - Andesitic-dacitic stratovolcanoes
   - Ignimbrite eruptions (large-volume)
   - Provides Li source (volcanic rock weathering)
   - Provides heat (geothermal systems)

3. **Basin Formation** (Pliocene-Quaternary)
   - Extensional to transtensional basins
   - Closed drainage (endorheic basins)
   - Sediment fill: 100-1000 m
   - Evaporite sequences

**Climate Control**:
- **Aridity**: <200 mm/year precipitation
- **Evaporation**: >2000 mm/year potential evaporation
- **Temperature**: High diurnal range (freeze-thaw cycles)
- **Wind**: Strong westerlies (enhance evaporation)

### 3.2 Salar de Atacama, Chile — Forearc Basin Model

**Tectonic Setting**:
- **Basin type**: Forearc basin (between trench and volcanic arc)
- **Basin age**: Oligocene to Recent (30 Ma to present)
- **Basin area**: 3000 km²
- **Basin depth**: >1000 m sediment fill

**Structural Framework**:
- **Western boundary**: Coastal Cordillera (Paleozoic basement)
- **Eastern boundary**: Pre-Cordillera (Mesozoic-Cenozoic fold-thrust belt)
- **Basin fill**: Alluvial fans + lacustrine + evaporites
- **Fault control**: NNE-trending normal faults

**Li Source and Transport**:

1. **Primary Sources**:
   - Volcanic rocks (andesites, dacites, ignimbrites)
   - Geothermal fluids (deep circulation)
   - Weathering of volcanic glass

2. **Transport Mechanisms**:
   - Surface runoff (ephemeral streams)
   - Groundwater flow (aquifer systems)
   - Geothermal upwelling (fault-controlled)

3. **Concentration Mechanisms**:
   - Evaporative concentration (>10,000× enrichment)
   - Halite precipitation (removes Na, Cl)
   - Mg-sulfate precipitation (removes Mg, SO₄)
   - **Result**: High Li/Mg ratio (Mg/Li = 6, world's best)

**Brine Characteristics**:
- Li concentration: 1400-2400 mg/L (average 1500 mg/L)
- Mg/Li ratio: 6.0-6.5 (optimal for extraction)
- TDS: 300-350 g/L
- Brine volume: ~200 km³

**Production Economics**:
- Lowest production cost globally (~$3000-4000/t LCE)
- Solar evaporation ponds (18-24 months cycle)
- Direct lithium carbonate production

### 3.3 Argentina Puna Plateau — Intramontane Basin Model

**Tectonic Setting**:
- **Basin type**: Intramontane basins (between volcanic ranges)
- **Basin age**: Pliocene to Recent (5 Ma to present)
- **Basin area**: Multiple small basins (10-500 km² each)
- **Elevation**: 3800-4200 m

**Key Salars**:

1. **Salar de Olaroz-Cauchari**
   - Basin area: 220 km²
   - Structural control: NW-trending fault zone
   - Li concentration: 600-900 mg/L
   - Mg/Li ratio: 3.5-4.5 (excellent)
   - Resource: ~3 Mt LCE

2. **Salar de Hombre Muerto**
   - Basin area: 585 km²
   - Structural control: Pull-apart basin
   - Li concentration: 600-800 mg/L
   - Mg/Li ratio: 1.4-1.8 (world's second best)
   - Resource: ~6 Mt LCE

3. **Salar de Rincon**
   - Basin area: 280 km²
   - Li concentration: 300-500 mg/L
   - Mg/Li ratio: 8-12

**Tectonic Controls on Brine Quality**:
- **Fault-controlled geothermal input**: Enhances Li leaching from volcanic rocks
- **Basin closure**: Prevents brine dilution
- **Volcanic glass abundance**: Primary Li source

### 3.4 Bolivia Uyuni — Mega-Basin Model

**Tectonic Setting**:
- **Basin type**: Altiplano mega-basin
- **Basin area**: 10,582 km² (world's largest salt flat)
- **Basin depth**: >100 m salt crust + >900 m sediment
- **Elevation**: 3656 m

**Brine Characteristics**:
- Li concentration: 200-500 mg/L (lower than Chile/Argentina)
- Mg/Li ratio: 15-25 (high, unfavorable)
- K concentration: High (potash co-product potential)
- Resource: ~21 Mt LCE (world's largest, but low grade)

**Why High Mg/Li Ratio?**:
1. **Basin closure**: Extremely closed system
2. **Long residence time**: Quaternary to Recent (>1 Ma)
3. **Mg accumulation**: Continuous Mg input from weathering
4. **Incomplete evaporation**: Large basin size limits evaporation efficiency

**Extraction Challenges**:
- High Mg/Li ratio requires extensive pre-treatment
- Cold climate (freeze-thaw cycles disrupt ponds)
- Political/social constraints (indigenous rights)

### 3.5 Tibetan Plateau, China — Carbonate-Type Brine Model

**Tectonic Setting**:
- **Geodynamic driver**: India-Eurasia collision (50 Ma to present)
- **Plateau uplift**: 5-10 Ma (rapid uplift phase)
- **Basin type**: Intramontane rift basins
- **Elevation**: 4000-5000 m

**Key Salars**:

1. **Zabuye (扎布耶)**
   - Basin type: Carbonate-type (unique globally)
   - Li concentration: 1500-2000 mg/L
   - Mg/Li ratio: 0.002-0.1 (world's best!)
   - K concentration: High (potash co-product)
   - Resource: ~1.5 Mt LCE

**Why Carbonate-Type?**:
- **Geothermal CO₂ input**: Fault-controlled CO₂ degassing
- **Carbonate precipitation**: Removes Mg as MgCO₃
- **Result**: Ultra-low Mg/Li ratio

2. **Qarhan (察尔汗)**
   - Basin type: Sulfate-type
   - Li concentration: 200-400 mg/L
   - Mg/Li ratio: 30-50 (high)
   - Resource: ~7 Mt LCE (large but low quality)

**Structural Controls**:
- **Rift faults**: Control geothermal fluid upwelling
- **Volcanic activity**: Provides Li source
- **Basin closure**: Prevents brine dilution

**Exploration Model for Tibetan Plateau**:
- Target: Rift basins with geothermal activity
- Geochemical indicator: High CO₂ flux (for carbonate-type)
- Structural control: Active fault zones

## Part IV: Clay-Type Lithium Deposits — Tectonic Controls

### 4.1 Basin and Range Province, USA — Extensional Basin Model

**Tectonic Setting**:
- **Province**: Basin and Range (Nevada, Oregon, California)
- **Tectonic regime**: Extensional (since 20 Ma)
- **Basin type**: Half-graben basins
- **Volcanic activity**: Miocene to Pliocene (20-5 Ma)

**Key Deposit: Thacker Pass (Nevada)**

**Geological Setting**:
- **Host rock**: McDermitt Caldera volcanic sequence (16.5 Ma)
- **Lithology**: Rhyolitic tuff and ash
- **Alteration**: Zeolite + smectite clay alteration
- **Depositional environment**: Alkaline lacustrine

**Tectonic Evolution**:
```
T₀: Caldera eruption (16.5 Ma) → Rhyolitic tuff deposition
T₁: Caldera collapse → Basin formation
T₂: Alkaline lake development (16-15 Ma)
T₃: Volcanic ash alteration → Li-rich clay formation
T₄: Basin infill and burial
```

**Li Enrichment Mechanism**:
1. **Volcanic glass dissolution**: Li released from rhyolitic glass
2. **Alkaline lake chemistry**: High pH (>9) enhances Li mobility
3. **Clay authigenesis**: Li adsorbed onto smectite clays
4. **Evaporative concentration**: Closed basin concentrates Li

**Deposit Characteristics**:
- Li grade: 0.2-0.4% Li (as clay-bound Li)
- Mineralogy: Hectorite (Li-smectite)
- Thickness: 50-150 m
- Resource: >15 Mt LCE (world's largest clay-type)

**Structural Controls**:
- **Caldera ring faults**: Control basin geometry
- **Basin-bounding faults**: Control sediment distribution
- **Geothermal systems**: Enhance alteration

### 4.2 Sierra Madre Occidental, Mexico — Volcanic Plateau Model

**Tectonic Setting**:
- **Province**: Sierra Madre Occidental volcanic belt
- **Volcanic activity**: Oligocene to Miocene (30-20 Ma)
- **Tectonic regime**: Back-arc extension
- **Basin type**: Intermontane basins

**Key Deposit: Sonora Clay Deposit**

**Geological Setting**:
- **Host rock**: Rhyolitic ignimbrite
- **Alteration**: Zeolite + clay alteration
- **Depositional environment**: Lacustrine
- **Li grade**: 0.15-0.25% Li

**Exploration Model for Clay-Type Deposits**:
- Target: Cenozoic volcanic basins with alkaline lacustrine sequences
- Lithology: Rhyolitic tuff/ash (high Li content in glass)
- Geochemical indicator: Li >500 ppm in altered tuff
- Mineralogy: Hectorite, smectite, zeolite assemblage

## Part V: Tectono-Magmatic-Metallogenic Relationships

### 5.1 Hard-Rock Lithium — Temporal Patterns

**Global Metallogenic Epochs**:

1. **Late Archean (2.7-2.6 Ga)**
   - Tectonic setting: Post-Kenoran Orogeny extension
   - Provinces: Yilgarn (Australia), Superior (Canada)
   - Pegmatite type: Greenstone-hosted LCT
   - Examples: Greenbushes, Whabouchi, Rose

2. **Mesoproterozoic (1.0-0.95 Ga)**
   - Tectonic setting: Kibaran intracontinental orogeny
   - Provinces: Central Africa (DRC, Rwanda, Zimbabwe)
   - Pegmatite type: Metasediment-hosted LCT
   - Examples: Manono, Arcadia, Bikita

3. **Paleozoic (380-310 Ma)**
   - Tectonic setting: Variscan/Alleghanian orogenies
   - Provinces: Appalachians (USA), Bohemian Massif (Europe)
   - Pegmatite type: S-type granite-related LCT
   - Examples: Kings Mountain, Cinovec

4. **Mesozoic (220-200 Ma)**
   - Tectonic setting: Indosinian Orogeny (Triassic collision)
   - Provinces: Songpan-Ganzi (China)
   - Pegmatite type: Two-mica granite-related LCT
   - Examples: Jiajika, Ke'eryin

**Common Tectonic Denominators**:
- Post-orogenic extensional setting (20-50 Ma after peak collision)
- S-type granite magmatism (crustal melting)
- High degree of fractionation (>95% crystallization)
- Emplacement along extensional structures

### 5.2 Brine-Type Lithium — Temporal Patterns

**Metallogenic Timing**:
- **Dominant age**: Quaternary (2.6 Ma to present)
- **Older examples**: Pliocene (5-2.6 Ma) in some Argentine salars

**Tectonic Prerequisites**:
1. **Plateau uplift**: Creates high-elevation closed basins
2. **Volcanic arc activity**: Provides Li source
3. **Arid climate**: Enables evaporative concentration
4. **Fault activity**: Controls basin geometry and geothermal input

**Why Quaternary?**:
- Andean plateau reached critical elevation (>3500 m) in Pliocene
- Climate became sufficiently arid in Quaternary
- Active volcanism provides continuous Li input

### 5.3 Clay-Type Lithium — Temporal Patterns

**Metallogenic Timing**:
- **Dominant age**: Miocene to Pliocene (20-5 Ma)
- **Tectonic setting**: Extensional basins with active volcanism

**Formation Requirements**:
1. **Explosive volcanism**: Produces abundant volcanic glass
2. **Alkaline lake environment**: High pH (>9) for Li mobility
3. **Closed basin**: Prevents Li loss
4. **Sufficient time**: 1-5 Ma for alteration and concentration

## Part VI: Structural Controls on Mineralization

### 6.1 Regional-Scale Structural Controls

**Hard-Rock Deposits**:

1. **Orogenic Belts**:
   - Control distribution of fertile granites
   - Provide heat source for crustal melting
   - Examples: Yilgarn, Superior, Songpan-Ganzi

2. **Major Shear Zones**:
   - Act as magma conduits
   - Control pegmatite field distribution
   - Spacing: 10-100 km
   - Examples: Greenbushes Shear Zone, La Grande Shear Zone

3. **Craton Margins**:
   - Preferential sites for post-orogenic extension
   - Juxtapose fertile metasediments and granites
   - Examples: Yilgarn Craton margin, Congo Craton margin

**Brine Deposits**:

1. **Subduction Zones**:
   - Control volcanic arc position
   - Provide Li source (volcanic rocks)
   - Example: Andean subduction zone

2. **Rift Systems**:
   - Create closed basins
   - Control geothermal fluid circulation
   - Examples: Tibetan Plateau rifts, Basin and Range

3. **Plateau Margins**:
   - Optimal sites for closed basin formation
   - Balance between uplift and subsidence
   - Examples: Altiplano-Puna margin

### 6.2 Deposit-Scale Structural Controls

**Hard-Rock Deposits**:

1. **Fault Zones**:
   - Control pegmatite emplacement
   - Orientation: Typically extensional (normal faults)
   - Dip: 45-90° (steep)

2. **Fold Structures**:
   - Hinge zones: Preferential pegmatite sites
   - Axial planes: Control pegmatite orientation
   - Example: Pilgangoora Syncline

3. **Lithological Contacts**:
   - Competency contrasts focus stress
   - Create dilatational sites
   - Example: Greenstone-granite contacts (Greenbushes)

**Brine Deposits**:

1. **Basin-Bounding Faults**:
   - Control basin geometry and size
   - Control groundwater inflow
   - Example: Atacama Basin faults

2. **Intra-Basin Faults**:
   - Control geothermal fluid upwelling
   - Enhance Li leaching from volcanic rocks
   - Example: Hombre Muerto fault system

3. **Caldera Ring Faults**:
   - Control basin subsidence
   - Focus geothermal activity
   - Example: McDermitt Caldera (Thacker Pass)

## Part VII: Predictive Exploration Models

### 7.1 Hard-Rock Lithium Exploration Model

**Regional-Scale Targeting**:

**Step 1: Identify Fertile Orogenic Belts**
- Age: Archean to Mesozoic
- Tectonic setting: Post-orogenic extension
- Granite type: S-type, two-mica granites
- Geochemical signature: High Li-Cs-Ta-Be

**Step 2: Map Structural Corridors**
- Major shear zones (10-100 km spacing)
- Extensional fault systems
- Greenstone-granite contacts

**Step 3: Geochemical Reconnaissance**
- Stream sediment sampling (Li-Cs-Rb-Ta)
- Soil sampling in prospective areas
- Anomaly threshold: Li >50 ppm (stream sediment)

**Step 4: Geophysical Surveys**
- Magnetics: Pegmatites show magnetic lows
- Gravity: Pegmatites show gravity lows (low density)
- Radiometrics: K/Th ratio anomalies

**Deposit-Scale Targeting**:

**Step 1: Detailed Geological Mapping**
- Map pegmatite dyke swarms
- Identify structural controls
- Assess pegmatite zoning

**Step 2: Geochemical Sampling**
- Rock chip sampling (Li-Cs-Ta-Be-Sn)
- Spodumene identification (XRD)
- Anomaly threshold: Li₂O >0.5%

**Step 3: Drilling**
- Target: Intermediate to core zones
- Spacing: 50-100 m (initial), 25 m (infill)
- Depth: 100-500 m (typical pegmatite depth)

**Key Exploration Criteria**:
- S-type granite proximity (<5 km)
- Shear zone/fault control
- Pegmatite swarm (multiple dykes)
- Spodumene-bearing assemblage
- Li₂O grade >1.0% (economic threshold)

### 7.2 Brine-Type Lithium Exploration Model

**Regional-Scale Targeting**:

**Step 1: Identify Favorable Tectonic Settings**
- Active/recent volcanic arcs
- High-elevation plateaus (>3000 m)
- Extensional/transtensional basins
- Arid climate (precipitation <200 mm/year)

**Step 2: Map Closed Basins**
- Endorheic drainage (no outlet)
- Basin area: 10-1000 km²
- Evaporite presence (salt flats)

**Step 3: Assess Volcanic Li Source**
- Volcanic rock type: Andesite, dacite, rhyolite
- Volcanic glass abundance
- Geothermal activity (hot springs)

**Step 4: Preliminary Brine Sampling**
- Surface brine or shallow wells (<10 m)
- Analyze: Li, Mg, K, Na, Ca, SO₄, Cl
- Target: Li >200 mg/L, Mg/Li <10

**Deposit-Scale Evaluation**:

**Step 1: Geophysical Surveys**
- TEM (Transient Electromagnetics): Map brine-bearing aquifers
- Gravity: Map basin depth and structure
- Seismic: Map sediment stratigraphy

**Step 2: Drilling Program**
- Drill spacing: 1-5 km (regional), 500 m (detailed)
- Depth: 100-500 m (typical aquifer depth)
- Core/cuttings: Analyze lithology and porosity

**Step 3: Brine Sampling and Analysis**
- Sample from multiple aquifer levels
- Analyze: Li, Mg, K, Na, Ca, SO₄, Cl, B
- Pumping tests: Assess aquifer productivity

**Step 4: Resource Estimation**
- Aquifer volume (from geophysics + drilling)
- Brine grade (Li concentration)
- Porosity (from core analysis)
- Resource = Volume × Porosity × Grade × Recovery

**Key Exploration Criteria**:
- Closed basin with evaporites
- Volcanic rock source (Quaternary)
- Li concentration >300 mg/L
- Mg/Li ratio <10 (preferably <5)
- Aquifer thickness >50 m
- Porosity >15%

### 7.3 Clay-Type Lithium Exploration Model

**Regional-Scale Targeting**:

**Step 1: Identify Volcanic Provinces**
- Age: Cenozoic (Oligocene to Pliocene)
- Volcanic type: Rhyolitic (high Li in glass)
- Tectonic setting: Extensional basins

**Step 2: Map Lacustrine Basins**
- Basin type: Closed, alkaline lakes
- Basin size: 10-500 km²
- Sediment type: Tuffaceous, zeolitic

**Step 3: Geochemical Reconnaissance**
- Sample altered volcanic rocks
- Target: Li >500 ppm in tuff/ash
- Mineralogy: Hectorite, smectite, zeolite

**Deposit-Scale Evaluation**:

**Step 1: Detailed Mapping**
- Map volcanic stratigraphy
- Identify alteration zones
- Sample systematically (100-500 m spacing)

**Step 2: Drilling Program**
- Drill spacing: 500 m (initial), 100 m (infill)
- Depth: 50-200 m (typical clay zone depth)
- Core: Analyze Li grade and mineralogy

**Step 3: Metallurgical Testing**
- Leach tests (acid or alkaline)
- Li recovery rates
- Impurity levels (Mg, Ca, Fe)

**Key Exploration Criteria**:
- Rhyolitic volcanic host
- Alkaline lacustrine environment
- Li grade >0.2% (economic threshold)
- Hectorite/smectite mineralogy
- Thickness >20 m
- Low impurities (Mg, Ca)

## Part VIII: Case Studies

### 8.1 Greenbushes, Australia — Archean Shear Zone-Hosted Pegmatite

**Tectonic Setting**:
- **Craton**: Yilgarn Craton (Archean)
- **Orogeny**: Late Archean (2.7-2.6 Ga)
- **Pegmatite age**: 2527 ± 2 Ma (U-Pb zircon)

**Structural Controls**:
- **Regional structure**: Donnybrook-Bridgetown Shear Zone (NNW-trending)
- **Local structure**: Greenbushes Shear Zone (subsidiary)
- **Host rocks**: Archean greenstones (mafic-ultramafic)
- **Granite proximity**: Donnybrook Granite (2 km west)

**Pegmatite Characteristics**:
- **Geometry**: 3.5 km long, 50-250 m wide, >400 m depth
- **Dip**: 60-80° east
- **Zoning**: Well-developed (border → wall → intermediate → core)
- **Mineralogy**: Spodumene + lepidolite + tantalite

**Genetic Model**:
1. Late Archean collision → crustal thickening
2. Post-orogenic extension → S-type granite intrusion (Donnybrook Granite)
3. Granite fractionation → LCT pegmatite melt
4. Emplacement along Greenbushes Shear Zone (extensional jog)
5. Rapid crystallization → spodumene + lepidolite

**Exploration Lessons**:
- Target greenstone-granite contacts
- Focus on major shear zones
- Look for pegmatite swarms (multiple dykes)
- Spodumene + lepidolite = high-grade target

### 8.2 Salar de Atacama, Chile — Forearc Basin Brine Deposit

**Tectonic Setting**:
- **Plate boundary**: Nazca-South America subduction zone
- **Basin type**: Forearc basin
- **Basin age**: Oligocene to Recent (30 Ma to present)
- **Volcanic arc**: Active Andean arc (50 km east)

**Structural Controls**:
- **Western boundary**: Coastal Cordillera (Paleozoic basement)
- **Eastern boundary**: Pre-Cordillera (fold-thrust belt)
- **Basin faults**: NNE-trending normal faults
- **Geothermal systems**: Fault-controlled (El Tatio, etc.)

**Brine Formation Model**:
1. Volcanic rock weathering → Li release
2. Groundwater transport → Li to basin
3. Geothermal input → Enhanced Li leaching
4. Evaporative concentration → Li enrichment (>1500 mg/L)
5. Halite + Mg-sulfate precipitation → Mg removal (Mg/Li = 6)

**Why World's Best Brine?**:
- **Optimal Mg/Li ratio**: Mg-sulfate precipitation removes Mg
- **High Li concentration**: Continuous volcanic input + long residence time
- **Large resource**: 200 km³ brine volume
- **Low production cost**: Solar evaporation + direct carbonate production

**Exploration Lessons**:
- Target forearc basins adjacent to active volcanic arcs
- Look for geothermal activity (fault-controlled)
- Assess Mg/Li ratio early (critical for economics)
- Large basin size = large resource potential

### 8.3 Thacker Pass, USA — Caldera-Hosted Clay Deposit

**Tectonic Setting**:
- **Province**: Basin and Range (extensional)
- **Volcanic center**: McDermitt Caldera (16.5 Ma)
- **Tectonic regime**: Back-arc extension
- **Basin type**: Caldera collapse basin

**Structural Controls**:
- **Caldera ring faults**: Control basin geometry
- **Basin-bounding faults**: Control sediment distribution
- **Geothermal systems**: Enhance alteration

**Clay Formation Model**:
1. Caldera eruption (16.5 Ma) → Rhyolitic tuff deposition
2. Caldera collapse → Basin formation
3. Alkaline lake development → High pH (>9)
4. Volcanic glass dissolution → Li release
5. Clay authigenesis → Li adsorption onto hectorite
6. Evaporative concentration → Li enrichment (0.2-0.4%)

**Deposit Characteristics**:
- **Host**: Altered rhyolitic tuff
- **Mineralogy**: Hectorite (Li-smectite) + zeolite
- **Grade**: 0.2-0.4% Li (3000-6000 ppm)
- **Thickness**: 50-150 m
- **Resource**: >15 Mt LCE

**Exploration Lessons**:
- Target Cenozoic calderas with alkaline lacustrine sequences
- Look for rhyolitic tuff/ash (high Li in glass)
- Hectorite mineralogy = favorable for extraction
- Large, flat-lying deposits = low-cost mining

## Part IX: Summary and Recommendations

### 9.1 Key Tectonic Controls — Summary

**Hard-Rock (LCT Pegmatite)**:
- **Tectonic setting**: Post-orogenic extension (20-50 Ma after collision)
- **Magmatic source**: S-type granite (crustal melting)
- **Structural control**: Shear zones, extensional faults, lithological contacts
- **Temporal pattern**: Episodic (Archean, Proterozoic, Paleozoic, Mesozoic)
- **Global distribution**: Orogenic belts worldwide

**Brine (Salt Lake)**:
- **Tectonic setting**: Plateau/rift basins with active volcanism
- **Li source**: Volcanic rock weathering + geothermal fluids
- **Structural control**: Basin-bounding faults, geothermal fault systems
- **Temporal pattern**: Quaternary (continuous formation)
- **Global distribution**: Andean Plateau, Tibetan Plateau, Basin and Range

**Clay (Sedimentary)**:
- **Tectonic setting**: Extensional basins with explosive volcanism
- **Li source**: Volcanic glass dissolution in alkaline lakes
- **Structural control**: Caldera ring faults, basin-bounding faults
- **Temporal pattern**: Cenozoic (Oligocene to Pliocene)
- **Global distribution**: Basin and Range, Sierra Madre Occidental

### 9.2 Exploration Strategy Recommendations

**For Hard-Rock Lithium**:

1. **Regional Targeting**:
   - Focus on post-orogenic extensional belts
   - Prioritize S-type granite provinces
   - Map major shear zones and fault systems

2. **Geochemical Exploration**:
   - Stream sediment surveys (Li-Cs-Rb-Ta)
   - Soil sampling in prospective areas
   - Anomaly follow-up with rock chip sampling

3. **Geophysical Surveys**:
   - Magnetics (pegmatite = magnetic low)
   - Gravity (pegmatite = gravity low)
   - Radiometrics (K/Th ratio anomalies)

4. **Drilling Strategy**:
   - Target pegmatite swarms (multiple dykes)
   - Focus on intermediate to core zones
   - Assess grade, mineralogy, and continuity

**For Brine-Type Lithium**:

1. **Regional Targeting**:
   - Focus on high-elevation closed basins (>3000 m)
   - Prioritize areas with active/recent volcanism
   - Assess climate (arid = favorable)

2. **Basin Evaluation**:
   - Map basin geometry (geophysics)
   - Sample surface brines or shallow wells
   - Assess Li concentration and Mg/Li ratio

3. **Drilling Program**:
   - Drill to assess aquifer depth and thickness
   - Sample brines from multiple levels
   - Conduct pumping tests

4. **Resource Estimation**:
   - Integrate geophysics, drilling, and brine chemistry
   - Calculate: Volume × Porosity × Grade × Recovery
   - Assess production costs (Mg/Li ratio critical)

**For Clay-Type Lithium**:

1. **Regional Targeting**:
   - Focus on Cenozoic volcanic provinces
   - Prioritize rhyolitic calderas
   - Map alkaline lacustrine basins

2. **Geochemical Exploration**:
   - Sample altered volcanic rocks
   - Target: Li >500 ppm in tuff/ash
   - Identify hectorite/smectite mineralogy

3. **Drilling Program**:
   - Drill to assess clay zone thickness and grade
   - Core for detailed mineralogy and metallurgy
   - Assess continuity and homogeneity

4. **Metallurgical Testing**:
   - Leach tests (acid or alkaline)
   - Assess Li recovery rates
   - Evaluate impurity levels

- Central Asia (Kazakhstan, Kyrgyzstan): Paleozoic orogenic belts
